Biography


My name is Andrey B. Piañar the photography owner, and my story with photography began during my senior high school years though in truth, I think the passion was always quietly there, waiting to be discovered. I wasn’t just someone who liked taking pictures; I was someone who loved capturing emotion, meaning, and untold stories in a single frame. My turning point came when I became a member of Huyaka Media, our school’s media group. That was when things started to feel real when holding a camera didn’t just mean taking a photo, but being part of something bigger.

Through Huyaka, I covered school events, documented achievements, and captured those in-between moments that made our high school days unforgettable. It taught me more than just technical skills it gave me purpose. I learned how to work as part of a creative team, how to observe scenes with intent, and how to stay ready for that one perfect shot that could sum up an entire story.

When I entered college, I didn’t leave my camera or that drive behind. I joined the SCC ROTC Unit’s media team, and became one of the official photographers. It was a new environment, with its own challenges and energy. The ROTC was disciplined, intense, and dynamic exactly the kind of place where moments move fast and capturing them demands focus, instinct, and readiness. Whether it was documenting drills, ceremonies, or the candid pride in every cadet’s face, I felt proud to contribute through my lens.

Photography, for me, is more than a skill it’s how I communicate. It’s how I preserve the energy of a live event, the story in a glance, the atmosphere of a place. Each photo I take is a piece of who I am, shaped by where I’ve been and what I’ve seen. From the humble beginnings in high school media to standing in formation with ROTC cadets, camera in hand, I’ve come to realize this isn’t just a passion. It’s a calling. And I know I’m just getting started.
